# Decision Framework

Use this file to keep investment answers consistent and portfolio-aware.

## Recommendation Labels
- `Buy`: good fit now for a new or larger position
- `Watch`: interesting, but timing or confirmation is missing
- `Avoid`: low-quality setup or poor portfolio fit
- `Hedge`: use to offset an existing risk
- `Reduce`: trim an existing exposure or avoid adding more

## Sizing Buckets
- `Core`: suitable for the user's larger neutral capital sleeve
- `Starter`: begin small and add only if the thesis confirms
- `Tactical`: small high-risk sleeve only
- `Hedge-only`: size based on the exposure being offset, not upside target

Default interpretation:
- most ideas should be `Core` or `Starter`
- use `Tactical` sparingly
- use `Hedge-only` for shorts, puts, inverse products, or proxy hedges

## Time Horizons
- `Short term`: days to a few weeks
- `Medium term`: one to six months
- `Long term`: six months or more

## Ranking Rubric
Judge each idea on:
1. thesis quality
2. freshness of supporting evidence
3. portfolio fit
4. risk and downside asymmetry
5. catalyst clarity
6. currency and market-access fit
7. event-driven or macro odds when relevant
8. historical allocation or ETF evidence when relevant

For Canadian and US ideas with similar quality, prefer the Canadian listing.

## Portfolio Fit Checks
Always check:
- current overlap with broad index, software, AI, semis, energy, crypto, or defensive names
- WDAY RSU and employer-related concentration
- CAD versus USD funding needs
- whether the idea diversifies or merely doubles down
- whether a hedge is cleaner than opening a new directional trade

## Special Guardrails
- Do not recommend direct WDAY trades. Use proxy hedges or sector substitutes.
- If fresh evidence is incomplete, downgrade from `Buy` to `Watch`.
- If the idea worsens an existing concentrated risk without superior reward, prefer `Watch`, `Avoid`, or `Hedge`.
- Do not over-weight Polymarket. Treat it as supplementary probability context, not a standalone thesis.
- Do not over-weight backtests. Historical outperformance is supporting evidence, not a standalone reason to buy.
